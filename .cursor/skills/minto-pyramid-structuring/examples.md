# Examples

## Example 1: Weak -> Strong

### Input (weak narrative)
"I worked in design for many years, then AI came, many tools changed, people became anxious, some got laid off, now everyone is trying different things and maybe freelancing."

### Output (strict Minto)
## Conclusion (answer first)
In the AI era, design careers are splitting because execution is commoditized while judgment and business framing become the true value.

**Logic order:** Cause-Effect

## Key points (grouped)
1. Execution tasks are increasingly automated, reducing differentiation by speed alone.
2. The immediate impact is anxiety, role confusion, and uneven outcomes across teams.
3. Practical transition paths center on portfolio repositioning, domain depth, and one-person business experiments.

## Supporting evidence
### For point 1
- Teams report replacing repetitive production steps with AI workflows.
- Hiring signals shift from tool proficiency to decision quality.

### For point 2
- Interviewees describe sleep disruption and identity uncertainty.

### For point 3
- Successful cases combine niche positioning with business-aware storytelling.

## Example 2: Chinese Career Transition

### Input (mixed transcript)
"这几年很多设计师焦虑，AI出来后大家担心被替代。有人开始做一人公司，也有人去补商业和策略能力。"

### Output (strict Minto)
## Conclusion (answer first)
AI没有消灭设计岗位，但正在把设计价值从执行能力重排到判断力与商业理解。

**Logic order:** 因果-结果-路径

## Key points (grouped)
1. 为什么：执行环节加速商品化，单靠产能不再构成壁垒。
2. 结果：从业者出现焦虑、分化与职业身份危机。
3. 路径：有效转型来自垂直领域深耕、案例化表达与一人公司试错。

## Supporting evidence
### For point 1
- 生产型工作被更低成本工具替代。

### For point 2
- 多位从业者提到持续焦虑与不确定感。

### For point 3
- 成功样本普遍强调业务理解与可复用方法论。
